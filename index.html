01 The Origin

Complexity
is the Moat

Central Asia sits on hundreds of billions of barrels of proved and probable reserves, yet its energy markets remain the most fragmented, opaque, and underleveraged corridor on the planet.

EXETRION was born from a single thesis: where others see chaos, we see arbitrage. Where institutions see sovereign risk, we see a decade-long head start. Where legacy players see infrastructure gaps, we see vertical integration opportunities worth tens of billions.

On Day One, a single trade — 400,000 barrels of Fergana Valley crude at a $4.20 per barrel spread — generated $1.68 million in profit. The thesis was proved before the ink on the incorporation documents had dried.

03 The Journey

2026 — 2040

2026

The Trading Thesis

$4.2B notional volume. $40-60M profit. 18 employees. Offices in Tashkent, Geneva, and Dubai. EXETRION proves that complexity is the moat.

2027

First Acquisition

49% stake in a gas storage and compression facility in the Fergana Valley. $80M. The transition from trader to operator begins.

2028

Block F-7

30-year Production Sharing Agreement. 800 km². 150-250 million barrels of estimated reserves. London office opens to anchor European capital markets.

2029

First Oil

60,000 BOE per day. $3.8B revenue. 420 employees across five offices. The Fergana Valley crude begins to flow.

2030

NYSE IPO — EXTR

Raises $3.1 billion. Day One close: $61 per share. Market cap: ~$29 billion. EXETRION enters the global stage.

2031

Iron Corridor

640 km pipeline. $2.1 billion. Designed from day one with hydrogen conversion in mind — infrastructure that ages forward, not backward.

2032

Meridian NV

$4.8B acquisition. Dutch midstream: LNG terminals in Rotterdam and Zeebrugge, North Sea platforms, EU distribution in 9 countries. Houston trading desk opens.

2033

Permian Basin

$6-8B acquisition. 80,000 BOE/day, targeting 200,000 BOE/day. American shale meets Central Asian crude — a two-hemisphere production base.

2034

Gulf of Mexico & EXETRION Fuel

Deepwater JV. Retail fuel launch: 100 stations across TX, LA, OK — scaling to 2,000+ by 2040. Gas, diesel, hydrogen, EV charging at every pump.

2035

EXETRION Horizon

900MW wind farm. The renewables division launches with a target of 50GW by 2038 — solar, onshore, offshore wind. The energy transition, on EXETRION's terms.

2037

Hydrogen Corridor

The Iron Corridor pipeline is repurposed for green hydrogen export. Infrastructure designed in 2031 finds its ultimate purpose six years later.

2038

Fusion Alliance

22% stake in a private fusion company. Commercialization rights in 31 countries. The final frontier of energy — and EXETRION has a seat at the table.

2040

The Trillion-Dollar Vision

$1 trillion market cap. 38,000 employees. $90B revenue. 48 countries. 5 continents. From a single trade in Tashkent to the energy company that defined the century.
